Stir-Fry

Vegetable stir-fry is a quick and easy dish that involves cooking vegetables in a wok or large skillet over high heat with a small amount of oil. The vegetables are usually cut into bite-sized pieces and can include a variety of different vegetables such as broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, mushrooms, onions, and snap peas. The vegetables are typically cooked for a few minutes until they are tender-crisp, which means they still have a bit of crunch but are no longer raw.

Stir-fry dishes are often seasoned with soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and other flavorful ingredients, and can be served as a side dish or as a main dish over rice or noodles. The beauty of vegetable stir-fry is that it's versatile and can be customized to your taste by adding different vegetables, sauces, or proteins such as tofu, chicken, or shrimp. It's a healthy and delicious way to get a variety of colorful vegetables into your diet.

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 2 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Directions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, and cornstarch. Set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the sliced bell peppers and onion to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes until they begin to soften.
  4. Add the sliced zucchini, yellow squash, and minced garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes.
  5. Pour the soy sauce mixture over the vegetables and stir to coat.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens and the vegetables are cooked to your liking.
  7. Serve the stir-fry hot over rice or noodles. Enjoy!
